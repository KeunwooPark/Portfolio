Keunwoo Park

Keunwoo Park

I am the chief product officer at Ordinary Magic Corp., a company based in South Korea, making toys for infants and toddlers under the Peekaby brand. Our team builds both hardware toys and software appliations. The toys are media between children and parents, and software applications are intelligent tools to improve the playing experience.


I completed my Ph.D. at KAIST HCIL. During my Ph.D., I foused on technologies for physical human-computer interaction. I developed technologies to 1) bring the existing interaction concepts closer to our life, and 2) support novel interaction ideas. My projects include smartwatch interactions, haptic devices, fabrication, and hand sensing technologies.


Prior to my Ph.D., I completed a bachelor's course in Mechanical Engineering and Computer Science in KAIST. I also finished a master's course for Computer Science in KAIST.

Why do I write my contributions for the papers that I'm not a first author?

Current authorship system is not transparent. We know that a first author did most of the things, but it is hard to know what the other authors did. Therefore, I manifest my contributions for the projects that I played supporting roles. I want to emphasize that HCI research projects are mostly collaborative work. A research project cannot be done by one person and contributions of non-first-authors are crucial. If you are intersted in the authorship issue, please read this interesting paper: Solving the battle of first-authorship: Using interactive technology to highlight contributions.
