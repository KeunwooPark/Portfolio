Keunwoo Park's Homepage

Keunwoo Park

I'm a Ph.D. student at KAIST HCIL. I'm interested in technologies for physical human-computer interaction. I develop technologies to 1) bring the existing interaction concepts closer to our life, and 2) support novel interaction ideas. My projects include smartwatch interactions, haptic devices, and multi-finger classifying and tracking technologies. Besides my research projects, I'm also interested in fabrication and tangible interaction.

I completed a bachelor's course in Mechanical Engineering and Computer Science in KAIST. I also finished a master's course for Computer Science in KAIST.

Why do I write my contributions for the papers that I'm not a first author?

Current authorship system is not transparent. We know that a first author did most of the things, but it is hard to know what the other authors did. Therefore, I manifest my contributions for the projects that I played supporting roles. I want to emphasize that HCI research projects are mostly collaborative work. A research project cannot be done by one person and contributions of non-first-authors are crucial. If you are intersted in the authorship issue, please read this interesting paper: Solving the battle of first-authorship: Using interactive technology to highlight contributions.
